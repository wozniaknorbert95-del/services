### § C — HOW IT WORKS (5 steps, HITL highlighted)

**Wireframe**
```
H2 — How Inbox Killer works
[1 Reads] → [2 Sorts] → [3 Prioritises] → [4 Drafts] → [5 You approve ✋]
                                                          ^ highlighted
one-line caption under each step
```

**Copy (final)**

> **H2:** How it works — five quiet steps.
>
> 1. **Reads** — it watches the inboxes you choose. Nothing else.
> 2. **Sorts** — every message lands in a clear lane: lead, client, invoice, or noise.
> 3. **Prioritises** — the messages that earn you money rise to the top.
> 4. **Drafts** — ready-to-send replies appear, written in your voice.
> 5. **You approve** ✋ — you send with one click. It never sends on its own.

**Rationale**
- Five short steps = "I understand this" in 10 seconds (premium clarity).
- Step 5 visually highlighted = HITL turned into the hero of the flow, not fine print → this is the moat (Strategy §8) made tangible.
- "in your voice" answers the silent objection "robotic replies will embarrass me".

### § H — FAQ (objection handling)

**Copy (final)**

> **Does it send emails on its own?**
> No. Every reply waits for your one-click approval. Approval-by-default is the core design.
>
> **Will it work with my email?**
> Yes — it connects to standard inboxes (the ones you already use). We confirm compatibility during your Automation Map.
>
> **Will the replies sound like me?**
> They're tuned to your voice during setup, and you refine them any time. You approve before anything goes out.
>
> **How long until it's running?**
> Most setups go live in days, not weeks — because the system is already built and proven; we adapt it to you.
>
> **Why is the Automation Map paid?**
> Because it's real work that delivers real value: we find your two or three biggest leaks and the ROI before you commit. The fee is credited toward your project.

**Rationale**
- FAQ = pre-empts the exact friction points that kill SMB conversions (control, compatibility, embarrassment, speed) + defends the paid-Discovery model (Strategy §5.1, §8).
